SHARP BETTING PRINCIPLES
Where data meets conviction — prove your edge against your crew
Based on Stanford Wong's Sharp Sports Betting
◆ Core Rules — The Non-Negotiables ▼
01
Only Bet With An Edge
If you have no edge, optimal bet size = $0. No edge means no bet. Every bet must have a quantifiable reason you expect positive EV. "I feel good about this one" is not an edge.
02
Know Your Break-Even
At -110, you need 52.38% winners to break even. Every bet has a break-even rate. If your estimated win probability doesn't exceed it, the bet is -EV. Period.
03
Size By Edge, Not Emotion
Use Kelly Criterion. Bigger edge = bigger bet. Bet multiplier = edge / MinEdge. Size to your MinWin, not a flat dollar amount. Never let a single weekend wipe your bankroll.
04
Line Shop Everything
Shop 12+ books before placing. The difference between -110 and -105 is 2.2% on action. Over 1,000 bets that's massive. Always buy through key numbers 3 and 7 when price is right.
05
Record Everything
Track: date, teams, your line estimate, actual line, result. Calculate net / total action = actual edge. If your "big edge" picks underperform after 100+ bets, they weren't big edge picks.

◆ Three Sources of Edge ▼

1. Breaking News

Act immediately — edge decays in minutes
  • Key player injury in practice (not public yet)
  • Food poisoning, illness affecting team
  • Coaching changes, suspensions
  • Weather shifts (totals especially)
  • Rule changes books haven't adjusted to
  • Stadium/political factors

2. Superior Data Processing

Best in illiquid markets, not NFL sides
  • Power ratings: assign strength, subtract for spread
  • Best opportunities: overnight lines, totals, props
  • Small-college / niche sports = softer lines
  • Track overcompensation after big TV wins/losses
  • Deep conference knowledge beats general models

3. Motivation / Situational

Lines set by numbers, motivation is lopsided
  • Teams that clinched — reduce home-field, fade them
  • Must-win games — teams outperform numbers
  • Horrible Defeat Syndrome — multi-week ATS slumps
  • Big dogs play hard, big favorites coast
  • NBA: intensity beats talent, especially playoffs

4. Fade Fan Money

Public sentiment = inflated lines on other side
  • Heavy public side → take unpopular side at value
  • Strongest: Super Bowl, NBA playoffs, marquee fights
  • Squares back favorites → books shade accordingly
  • Home dogs are historically undervalued
  • NFL home dogs +7 covered 52.7% over 16 years
◆ Pre-Bet Checklist — Go / No-Go ▼
  • ✓Estimated win probability exceeds break-even at offered terms
  • ✓Edge is quantifiable and ≥ MinEdge (recommended: 5%)
  • ✓Can articulate exactly WHY market is wrong
  • ✓Line shopped across 12+ books for best price
  • ✓Checked key number half-points (3, 7 in NFL)
  • ✓Compared ML vs spread for close games (±3.5)
  • ✓Bet sized by Kelly — edge/MinEdge × MinWin
  • ✓All simultaneous bets won't wipe bankroll on bad day
  • ✓For season bets: annualized ROI justifies capital lock-up
  • ✓Bet recorded with date, teams, line estimate, actual line
◆ Red Flags — Kill The Bet ▼
⚠
No Articulated Edge
If you can't explain why you have an edge in one sentence, you don't have one.
⚠
Big Favorite -400+
Need 80%+ win rate to break even. Almost always overpriced by fan money.
⚠
Teaser Missing Key Numbers
Only profitable if teased points cross BOTH the 3 and the 7. Otherwise -EV.
⚠
Parlay With "Ties Lose"
Destroys expected value. Pushes should return money, not lose the leg.
⚠
Season Bet < 2 Game Edge
For 6-month sports at -110, need projection to exceed prop by ≥2 games.
⚠
Emotional Attachment
Betting your team, chasing losses, or "gut feeling" without math = square behavior.
⚠
Insufficient Sample Size
60% win rate needs 250+ games to be statistically meaningful. Don't trust small samples.
⚠
Capital Lock-Up Without ROI
Futures tying up money 6 months need ≥15% expected return to justify.
◆ Money Management — Kelly Criterion ▼
$
Bankroll Rules
Bankroll = amount you'd stop betting if lost (not total wealth).
MinWin = 1.5–2.5% of bankroll at 5% MinEdge.
Rule of thumb: $200 MinWin per $10,000 bankroll.
Never let simultaneous bets wipe bankroll on bad weekend.

◆ Prop Bets — Poisson Analysis ▼
λ
Three-Step Process
1. Predict mean (from season data + matchup).
2. Apply Poisson distribution for each outcome probability.
3. Compare to offered odds — calculate edge.
POISSON APPLIES TO
  • ✓QB sacks, field goals, touchdowns (by count)
  • ✓Penalties by count, free throws made/missed
  • ✓Three-pointers, home runs, strikeouts
DOES NOT APPLY TO
  • ✗Yards (come in bunches)
  • ✗Points scored (2+ at a time in basketball)
  • ✗Penalty yards

◆ Statistical Significance — Trust Your Data ▼
σ
Use 1:1000 Rarity Minimum
Academic 1:100 (5%) is not enough — testing 100 hypotheses produces ~5 false positives. Use 1:1000 to filter. For data already played, hold to 1:10,000. Never use games that formed hypothesis to test it.

◆ Account Longevity — Don't Get Backed Off ▼
  • ●Don't pass on games too often — 100% bet-when-called ratio flags you as sharp
  • ●Make occasional -110 bets even when -105 isn't available
  • ●Don't exclusively bet only lowest-vig markets
  • ●Make bets natural relative to deposit size — small bets on large deposits look suspicious
  • ●One withdrawal per month looks normal — weekly withdrawals flag you
  • ●Never use two accounts at same book
  • ●Don't bet opposing sides to exploit bonuses — books ban "middling" for bonus value
