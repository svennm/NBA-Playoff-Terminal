2. Quick Start — Your First Bet in 2 Minutes

1
Create an account

Go to Slips. Enter a username and password (4+ characters). Click SIGN UP. You get $1,000 fake bankroll.

2
Pick a game and player

On the Slips page, select a game from the dropdown. Then select a player. Their prop lines appear — things like Points Over/Under 22.5.

3
Add to slip and bet

Click Over or Under on any line. It adds to your slip. Set a wager amount and hit LOCK IT IN. You can add multiple legs for a parlay.

4
Wait for the game to finish

Bets resolve automatically after games end. The system pulls box scores from ESPN, compares actual stats to your lines, and updates your bankroll.

Before placing a bet, check the Analysis page to see if our Poisson model finds any edge on the line you're looking at.

8. Understanding Odds & Lines

What is a Line?

A line is a number set on a player stat. Example: LeBron James Points O/U 25.5. You bet whether he'll score over or under 25.5 points. The .5 eliminates ties.

American Odds

OddsWhat It MeansImplied Probability
-110Bet $110 to win $10052.4% — standard line
-150Bet $150 to win $10060.0% — moderate favorite
-200Bet $200 to win $10066.7% — strong favorite
+100Bet $100 to win $10050.0% — even money
+150Bet $100 to win $15040.0% — underdog
+300Bet $100 to win $30025.0% — long shot

Quick formula: Negative odds → probability = |odds| / (|odds| + 100). Positive odds → probability = 100 / (odds + 100).

Parlay Math

A parlay combines multiple bets. ALL legs must hit to win. The odds multiply together.

Example: 3-leg parlay, each at -115 (implied 53.5%):

Combined probability: 53.5% x 53.5% x 53.5% = 15.3%

Payout: 1.87 x 1.87 x 1.87 = 6.54x your bet

What is Edge?

Edge = the difference between what you think the probability is and what the odds imply. If our model says Over is 58% likely, but the book odds imply only 52% — that's a +6% edge. Positive edge means the bet is profitable long-term.

9. How Our Lines Are Made (Poisson Model)

Every line on this platform is generated using the Poisson distribution — a statistical model for counting how many times something happens.

1
Get the average (mean)

Pull a player's game log for the selected window. Example: Jayson Tatum averaged 27.3 PTS over his last 10 games.

2
Set the line

Line = floor(mean) + 0.5. So 27.3 becomes 27.5. This is the over/under number.

3
Calculate probabilities

The Poisson formula calculates the probability of scoring 0, 1, 2... up to the line. Sum those up = probability of Under. The rest = probability of Over.

If mean = 27.3 and line = 27.5:

P(Under 27.5) = P(scoring 0) + P(scoring 1) + ... + P(scoring 27) = ~53%

P(Over 27.5) = 100% - 53% = ~47%

4
Convert to odds

53% Under becomes -113 odds. 47% Over becomes +113 odds.

Fit Quality

Poisson works best when variance roughly equals the mean. The platform checks this and labels each line:

ExcellentVariance is very close to the mean — model is reliable
GoodDecent fit — usable for analysis
FairSome divergence — use with caution
PoorVariance is far from mean — model may be unreliable for this stat

Rebounds and assists tend to fit Poisson well. Points are more variable (scored 2-3 at a time, not one-by-one) so the fit is usually "Good" not "Excellent".

Why Different Windows Give Different Lines

A player's average changes based on sample size. Example:

Season (72 games)24.824.5+108
Last 1028.128.5-112

This player is heating up. If a sportsbook still has their line at 24.5, the L5 data suggests the Over is much more likely than the season-based model predicts.

10. How Bets Get Resolved

After a game finishes, bets resolve automatically. Here's the process:

1System checks ESPN box scores for all finished games (every 5 minutes)
2Matches each bet leg to the player's actual stats
3Compares actual stat vs line (e.g. scored 28, line was 22.5 = Over hits)
4For parlays: ALL legs must hit. One miss = entire bet loses
5Updates bankroll and W/L record
