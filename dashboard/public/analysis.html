Poisson Model vs Book Lines
How This Works

For each player prop, we compute a Poisson probability from their game-by-game data, then convert it to fair American odds. We compare that against the book line to find the edge.

Edge % = (Model Prob - Implied Book Prob). Positive edge means the model thinks the book is giving you a better price than the true probability.

Poisson Fit — how well the player's data fits a Poisson distribution. Excellent and Good fits produce more reliable edges.

Dispersion — Var/Mean ratio. Poisson assumes = 1. Overdispersed (>1.5) means more variance than Poisson predicts — be cautious. Underdispersed (<0.7) means the player is very consistent.
